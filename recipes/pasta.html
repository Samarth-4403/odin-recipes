Pesto pasta recipe

Pesto pasta is a classic Italian dish with pasta coated in a flavorful sauce made from fresh basil, garlic, pine nuts, Parmesan cheese, and olive oil. It offers a harmonious blend of herbal, nutty, and cheesy flavors.

To make pesto pasta, start by cooking your choice of pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. While the pasta is cooking, prepare the pesto sauce. In a food processor or blender, combine fresh basil leaves, garlic cloves, pine nuts, grated Parmesan cheese, and a drizzle of olive oil. Pulse until the mixture forms a smooth paste. You can adjust the consistency by adding more olive oil if needed. Once the pasta is cooked, drain it and return it to the pot. Add the pesto sauce and toss until the pasta is well coated. Serve the pesto pasta hot, garnished with extra grated Parmesan cheese and a sprinkle of pine nuts, if desired. It's a simple yet delicious dish that celebrates the vibrant flavors of fresh basil.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces (225 grams) of pasta (such as spaghetti, linguine, or penne)
  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/3 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, prepare the pesto sauce. In a food processor or blender, combine fresh basil leaves, garlic cloves, pine nuts, grated Parmesan cheese, and a drizzle of olive oil.
  3. Pulse the ingredients until a smooth paste-like consistency is achieved. Adjust the consistency by adding more olive oil if needed. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Once the pasta is cooked, drain it and return it to the pot.
  5. Add the prepared pesto sauce to the cooked pasta and toss until the pasta is well coated.
  6. Serve the pesto pasta hot, garnished with extra grated Parmesan cheese and a sprinkle of pine nuts, if desired.
